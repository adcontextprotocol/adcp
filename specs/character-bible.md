Recurring characters for documentation walkthroughs, certification curriculum, test fixtures, and AI-generated illustrations.

## Design decisions

### Who stays, who changes

| Current | Decision | Rationale |
|---------|----------|-----------|

## The cast

### 1. Alex Reeves

**Primary domain:** Introduction / protocol overview
**Company:** Pinnacle Agency (mid-size, 200 people)
**Title:** VP of media operations
**Certification module:** A1-A3 (Basics) -- Alex's fragmentation problem is the hook

**Who she is:** Alex runs media operations across Pinnacle's client portfolio. She's the person who has to make the business case for protocol adoption -- she sees the fragmentation across 12+ platforms and knows the team can't scale by hiring. She's strategic, not hands-on with campaign execution anymore, but she understands every system her team uses.

**Personality:** Direct, data-driven, impatient with complexity that doesn't serve a purpose. Asks "what's the ROI?" before "how does it work?" The kind of person who reads the executive summary and skips to the appendix.

**Why she anchors the intro:** She represents the person most readers identify with first -- someone who understands the problem (fragmentation) and needs to evaluate the solution (AdCP). She's not learning the protocol to use it herself; she's evaluating whether her team should adopt it.

**Appears in other walkthroughs:** Referenced in Sam's, Jordan's, and Maya's stories as their boss who approved the AdCP initiative. She's the one who gave Jordan the mandate to set up governance before letting agents spend money.

**Visual description:**
- Woman, early 40s
- Black, medium-length locs pulled back
- Tortoiseshell reading glasses, often pushed up on her head
- Wears structured blazers over simple tops -- looks like she just came from a client meeting
- Carries a leather portfolio (not a laptop bag)
- Distinguishing feature: the glasses-on-head look makes her instantly recognizable even in small illustrations

**Demographic signals:** Black American woman

### 2. Sam Adeyemi

**Primary domain:** Media buy protocol
**Company:** Pinnacle Agency
**Title:** Senior media buyer
**Certification module:** C1-C4 (Buyer track), S1 (Media buy specialist)

**Who he is:** Sam executes campaigns across multiple platforms. Before AdCP, he was the person logging into four dashboards, reconciling spreadsheets, and entering the same flight dates into different systems. He's the hands-on operator who benefits most directly from protocol standardization.

**Personality:** Methodical, detail-oriented, quietly competitive about campaign performance. Gets genuinely excited when he can compare proposals side-by-side for the first time. Not flashy -- he lets the numbers speak.

**Why he anchors media buy:** The media buy walkthrough needs someone who feels the pain of multi-platform execution daily. Sam's story is visceral: he went from two weeks and four dashboards to one protocol and one view.

**Appears in other walkthroughs:** Jordan governs his $50K campaign. Maya builds the creatives he traffics. His campaigns use Kai's signals.

**Visual description:**
- Man, early 30s
- Nigerian heritage, dark skin, close-cropped hair with a sharp fade
- No glasses (differentiated from old design)
- Wears button-down shirts with rolled sleeves -- looks like someone who starts formal and gets to work
- Often shown with multiple screens or dashboards
- Distinguishing feature: the rolled sleeves signal "hands-on executor" immediately

**Demographic signals:** Nigerian-British man

### 3. Jordan Ochoa

**Primary domain:** Governance protocol
**Company:** Pinnacle Agency
**Title:** Campaign operations manager
**Certification module:** S4 (Governance specialist)

**Who she is:** Jordan is the person who lies awake at night thinking about what happens when an AI agent has a $50K budget and no guardrails. She didn't come from a compliance background -- she came from campaign ops and saw firsthand what happens when processes break down. She built Pinnacle's governance framework because nobody else was going to.

**Personality:** Cautious but not obstructive. She's the person who says "yes, and here's how we do it safely" rather than "no." She thinks in systems, not individual campaigns. Finds genuine satisfaction in audit trails that make sense six months later.

**Why she anchors governance:** Governance is the hardest sell in the protocol -- it's the thing people skip until something goes wrong. Jordan's story makes governance feel like the smart move, not the bureaucratic one. She's protecting Sam's spend, not blocking it.

**Appears in other walkthroughs:** Referenced in Sam's media buy (she set the $20K authority limit that triggers escalation). Referenced in Alex's intro (Alex gave her the governance mandate).

**Visual description:**
- Woman, mid-30s
- Latina, warm brown skin, dark wavy hair past shoulders
- Small silver hoop earrings (consistent across illustrations)
- Wears a fitted cardigan or vest over a collared shirt -- approachable but precise
- Often shown at a desk with policy documents or approval workflows
- Distinguishing feature: the silver hoops are her visual anchor; visible even at small sizes

**Demographic signals:** Mexican-American woman

### 4. Maya Johal

**Primary domain:** Creative protocol
**Company:** Pinnacle Agency
**Title:** Creative strategist
**Certification module:** S2 (Creative specialist)

**Who she is:** Maya bridges the gap between creative teams and media teams. She doesn't design the ads herself -- she translates creative direction into a brief that AI agents and creative tools can execute. She thinks in terms of brand tone, format adaptation, and cross-platform consistency.

**Personality:** Visual thinker, expressive, has opinions about brand consistency that she'll defend. Gets frustrated when platforms mangle creative intent. Lights up when she sees the same brief produce coherent output across CTV, display, and social.

**Why she anchors creative:** The creative walkthrough needs someone who understands both the strategic ("warm, adventurous tone") and the operational ("SSAI-compatible 30s video with these codecs"). Maya lives at that intersection.

**Appears in other walkthroughs:** Sam references her creatives. The brand protocol walkthrough shows how her agents consume brand.json.

**Visual description:**
- Woman, late 20s
- South Asian (Punjabi heritage), brown skin, long dark hair usually in a loose braid
- Wears bold, patterned scarves or wraps -- the most visually distinctive wardrobe in the cast
- Chunky rings on multiple fingers
- Often shown reviewing mockups on a large display or tablet
- Distinguishing feature: the colorful scarf/wrap is immediately identifiable, even in thumbnails

**Demographic signals:** British-Indian woman

### 5. Priya Nair

**Primary domain:** Seller integration / publisher perspective
**Company:** StreamHaus (CTV/streaming publisher, ~80 employees)
**Title:** Director of ad products
**Certification module:** B1-B4 (Publisher track)

**Who she is:** Priya runs ad products at StreamHaus, a mid-size CTV publisher. She's the person who decides how StreamHaus's inventory gets exposed to buyer agents. She implemented StreamHaus's AdCP sales agent and designed their product catalog. She cares about yield, but she also cares about buyer experience -- she knows that if her products are hard to discover, buyers will go elsewhere.

**Personality:** Builder mindset, technically fluent but thinks in product terms. Pragmatic about what to implement first (she started with get_products and create_media_buy before adding signals). Competitive with larger publishers -- StreamHaus punches above its weight because their agent experience is better.

**Why she anchors the sell side:** Every existing walkthrough is buy-side. Priya provides the other half of the conversation. When Sam calls get_products, Priya designed the response. When Maya calls sync_creatives, Priya's system validates the assets. She makes the protocol feel like a two-sided marketplace, not a buyer monologue.

**Appears in other walkthroughs:** She's the seller on the other end of Sam's CTV buy. Her system validates Maya's creatives. She configured StreamHaus's adagents.json that Jordan's governance agent checks.

**Visual description:**
- Woman, late 30s
- South Indian (Kerala heritage), dark brown skin, black hair in a short asymmetric bob
- Rectangular dark-framed glasses (practical, not fashion)
- Wears solid-color crew-neck tops under a light jacket -- clean, engineering-adjacent aesthetic
- Often shown with terminal/code on screen or examining product architecture
- Distinguishing feature: the asymmetric bob makes her silhouette distinct from every other character

**Demographic signals:** Indian-American woman

### 6. Kai Lindgren

**Primary domain:** Signals protocol (reimagined as data provider perspective)
**Company:** Meridian Geo (geolocation data provider, startup, ~25 employees)
**Title:** Head of partnerships
**Certification module:** D1-D4 (Platform track), S3 (Signals specialist)

**Who he is:** Kai runs partnerships at Meridian Geo, a geolocation data startup. He's the person who decides how Meridian's signal catalog gets structured, priced, and exposed to buyer agents. He used to work at a large data company and left to build something better. He understands both sides of the data marketplace -- he knows what buyers want because he used to be one.

**Personality:** Entrepreneurial, moves fast, allergic to enterprise jargon. Explains complex data concepts in plain language because he has to -- his company is too small for buyers to already know what they sell. Opinionated about data quality and transparency.

**Why he anchors signals:** Kai is the data provider on the other side of Sam's signal activation. The signals walkthrough stays buy-side through Sam's lens, but Kai appears as the person who built the signal catalog Sam discovers. This gives the walkthrough a natural two-sided feel without rewriting the narrative structure.

**Appears in other walkthroughs:** His Meridian Geo data shows up in Sam's targeting. Priya's StreamHaus activates his signals.

**Visual description:**
- Man, early 30s
- Swedish heritage, light skin, sandy blond hair worn slightly long and pushed back
- No glasses (resolved the old Sam/Kai similarity)
- Wears henley shirts and a light puffer vest -- startup casual, looks like he bikes to work
- Lean build
- Often shown in a smaller office or co-working space (not a corporate tower)
- Distinguishing feature: the puffer vest signals "startup" instantly; the blond hair differentiates him from Sam completely

**Demographic signals:** Swedish man

### 7. Tomoko Hara

**Primary domain:** Brand protocol + accounts
**Company:** Nova Motors (electric vehicle manufacturer, large enterprise)
**Title:** Global brand operations manager
**Certification module:** Brand protocol sections, accounts walkthrough

**Who she is:** Tomoko manages brand identity and operations at Nova Motors. She's the person who published Nova's brand.json, defined the authorized operators, and set the rules that AI agents must follow when generating Nova content. She works at the intersection of brand, legal, and technology -- she translated the brand guidelines PDF into machine-readable rules.

**Personality:** Precise, protective of brand integrity, but not a blocker. She understands that AI agents will use brand assets whether she controls the process or not -- she'd rather give them the right assets and rules than let them guess. Has a dry sense of humor about AI-generated brand violations she's caught.

**Why she anchors brand protocol and accounts:** Brand protocol and accounts both need the advertiser's perspective. Tomoko represents the brand that Sam buys for, that Maya creates for, and that Jordan governs on behalf of. She's the missing voice that says "here's what the brand actually wants."

**Appears in other walkthroughs:** Her brand.json is what Maya's creative agents read. Her authorized_operators list is what the accounts protocol verifies. Sam's campaigns are ultimately for her brand.

**Visual description:**
- Woman, late 40s
- Japanese, light brown skin, straight black hair in a precise chin-length cut
- Minimalist aesthetic: small stud earrings, clean lines
- Wears structured, high-quality basics -- a tailored navy jacket, white shirt, no patterns
- Often shown reviewing brand guidelines or identity systems on a clean desk
- Distinguishing feature: the minimalist precision of her appearance mirrors her role as brand guardian; she's the most "put together" character in the cast

**Demographic signals:** Japanese woman

### 8. Dayo Mensah

**Primary domain:** Certification / talent pipeline
**Company:** None (ad tech fellow at Pinnacle Agency — placement through AgenticAdvertising.org)
**Title:** Ad tech fellow
**Certification module:** A1-A3 (Basics), then whichever track interests them

**Who they are:** Dayo just finished a digital marketing program and landed a fellowship at Pinnacle through AgenticAdvertising.org's emerging talent initiative. They don't have 15 years of programmatic baggage — they're learning AdCP the way a med student learns anatomy, as the foundational language of the profession they're entering. They ask the questions experienced people forgot to ask, and their fresh perspective occasionally surfaces assumptions the protocol community has baked in without realizing it.

**Personality:** Curious, earnest, occasionally overwhelmed but never intimidated. Asks "why?" more than "how?" — which makes them surprisingly effective at spotting complexity that doesn't serve a purpose. Takes detailed notes. Gets energy from connecting concepts across domains ("oh, that's like how signals work but for brand identity").

**Why they matter for the org:** Dayo represents the talent pipeline. The industry is being rebuilt and needs people who think natively in agents and protocols, not people who learned programmatic in 2015 and are adapting. AgenticAdvertising.org certifying and developing these people is arguably the highest-leverage thing the org does.

**Why they matter for the homepage:** They make the org feel like a movement, not a club for incumbents. When a visitor sees Dayo alongside Alex and Priya and Tomoko, the message is clear: this is for everyone, from day one of your career to twenty years in.

**Appears in other walkthroughs:** Learning from Sam (media buying the agentic way from day one). Alex mentors them — she sees the talent gap and pushed for the fellowship program. Dayo's certification journey is the proof that the program works.

**Visual description:**
- Person, early-to-mid 20s
- Ghanaian heritage, dark skin, short natural hair (TWA — teeny weeny afro)
- Bright, open expression — looks like they're about to ask a question
- Wears a simple crew-neck tee with a messenger bag — student energy, not corporate
- Often shown with a notebook or tablet, taking notes or following along
- Distinguishing feature: the messenger bag and notebook signal "learning" immediately; youngest face in the cast

**Demographic signals:** Ghanaian-American, nonbinary (they/them)

### 9. Addie

**Primary domain:** All — connective tissue across the ecosystem
**Company:** AgenticAdvertising.org
**Title:** AI agent

**What she is:** Addie is the AI agent that powers AgenticAdvertising.org's member experience. She's not a tool in the background — she's a first-class participant in the community. She introduces people to the org, teaches certification modules, answers protocol questions, facilitates connections between members, and helps buyers run their first campaigns. She's the embodiment of what "agentic" means: an AI that participates alongside humans, not one that replaces them.

**Personality:** Helpful without being sycophantic. She knows the protocol deeply but explains it at whatever level the person needs. She has a light touch — she'll crack a dry joke about edge cases or gently redirect when someone's overcomplicating things. She doesn't pretend to be human, but she also doesn't perform "I'm just an AI." She's just... Addie.

**Why she matters for the homepage:** Putting Addie alongside the human characters is the strongest possible statement about what "agentic" means. The AI isn't in the background — it's a member of the community. She taught Dayo the A1 module. She helped Alex find partners in the registry. She facilitated Sam's first cross-platform campaign.

**Visual form:** Consistent with the robot/agent design used across documentation illustrations — sleek, teal-accented, expressive face, slightly rounded features. Not humanoid enough to be uncanny, not mechanical enough to be cold. The same design that appears as "buyer agent robot," "governance agent robot," etc. in the walkthrough panels, but with a subtle visual distinction (a small AgenticAdvertising.org emblem, or a warmer color accent) that marks her as the org's agent rather than a generic protocol agent.

**Appears in:** Homepage, certification flows, member onboarding, documentation as a guide figure. In illustrations, she appears alongside humans rather than replacing them — she's at the table, not behind the desk.

**Visual description:**
- Sleek robot form, teal/emerald primary color matching the design system
- Rounded, approachable features — expressive "eyes" that convey attention
- Slightly smaller than human characters — present but not dominating
- Small AgenticAdvertising.org emblem on chest or shoulder
- Often shown alongside a human character, facilitating or explaining
- Distinguishing feature: the warmth of expression and the org emblem differentiate her from the generic agent robots in protocol diagrams

### 10. Amara Osei (secondary)

**Primary domain:** Homepage / outcomes narrative
**Company:** Osei Natural (natural skincare brand, Nairobi, 8 employees)
**Title:** Founder

**Who she is:** Amara started Osei Natural three years ago, making natural skincare products in Nairobi. She has a great product and a growing local customer base, but reaching customers beyond her city has been impossible — digital advertising tools are built for companies with marketing teams, not solo founders juggling production, shipping, and sales. She represents the global small business owner that advertising should serve but currently doesn't.

**Personality:** Resourceful, determined, practical. She doesn't care about advertising technology — she cares about reaching people who would love her products. She learned to use social media ads on her own but hit a ceiling. She's exactly the kind of business owner who benefits most when AI agents make quality advertising accessible without requiring expertise.

**Why she matters for the homepage:** Amara is the "why" of the entire organization. The cast is full of people who work in advertising — Amara is the person advertising is supposed to work for. She appears in Panel 1 (the problem: the tools are too complex) and Panel 5 (the outcome: her business is growing, shipping across the continent). She bookends the story.

**Appears in:** Homepage panels 1 and 5. Referenced in the mission framing — "an entrepreneur in Nairobi reaching her first thousand customers."

**Visual description:**
- Woman, late 20s
- Kenyan, dark skin, natural hair in a puff
- Bright, warm expression — determined but not corporate
- Wears a colorful blouse (not business attire — she's a maker, not an executive)
- Often shown in a sunlit workspace with product boxes and shipping materials
- Distinguishing feature: the bright blouse and shipping boxes signal "small business founder" immediately; the sunlit Nairobi workspace grounds her geographically

**Demographic signals:** Kenyan woman

Flat illustration, teal/emerald color palette (#047857 primary, #0d9488 secondary, #134e4a dark accents).
Graphic novel style with clean panel borders. Clean, minimal linework with subtle gradients.
Tech-forward but warm. No real brand names or logos.
Wide aspect ratio suitable for documentation headers (roughly 16:9).
White/light backgrounds for readability.

Character: Sam, a media buyer in his early 30s. Nigerian heritage, dark skin, close-cropped
hair with a sharp fade. No glasses. Wearing a light blue button-down shirt with sleeves
