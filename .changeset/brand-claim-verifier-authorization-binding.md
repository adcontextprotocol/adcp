Close the `verify_brand_claim` / `verify_brand_claims` response-verifier brand-spoofing gap (NS-SIG-002). The designated-task response-signing profile required verifiers to check the JWS signature, `adcp_use`, freshness, and `request_hash`, but no step bound the signing key to the asserted `brand_domain`. Because `brand_domain` and `agent_url` are attacker-choosable fields inside the signed payload, an attacker running a conformant brand-agent for a domain it controls could emit a cryptographically valid `owned` envelope for any `brand_domain` — the signature attested authorship, not brand authority.

Adds a normative **Brand-authorization cross-check** verifier step to the security profile (`security.mdx`), mirroring the governance token's step-13 `iss`→brand.json binding and the request-signing agent-URL match: before trusting a signed envelope as evidence about `brand_domain`, an online verifier MUST resolve `{brand_domain}`'s `brand.json`, confirm a `type: "brand"` `agents[]` entry whose `url` byte-equals the envelope's `agent_url` (reject `response_signature_agent_not_in_brand_json` / `response_signature_brand_json_ambiguous`), and resolve the `kid` only within that entry's `jwks_uri`. The binding applies to both assertion and rejection directions. The `verify_brand_claim` and `verify_brand_claims` trust-model sections cross-reference the step, and a crypto-independent conformance vector set (`test-vectors/brand-response-signing/brand-authorization-cross-check.json`) covers the forged-owned, wrong-type, trailing-slash, ambiguous, forged-rejection, and authorized-accept cases.
