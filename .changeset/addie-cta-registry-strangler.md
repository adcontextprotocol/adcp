Strangler-fig refactor: the digest-newsletter `pickNudge` no longer maintains its own parallel rule list. Three cross-cut CTAs (cert continuation, find a working group, complete profile) now live in the suggested-prompts registry with both a `pull` facet (Addie home) and a new `digest` facet, each with its own typed `when` clause. Three digest-only CTAs (membership conversion, Slack onboarding, contributor cert) live in `DIGEST_ONLY_NUDGES`. `pickNudge` becomes a thin wrapper that merges both lists, filters by `digest.when`, and picks lowest priority. No behavior change — same 7 nudges, same priority order, same copy. The win is structural: one catalog, per-surface eligibility. Adding a new CTA used to mean editing both `prompt-rules.ts` and `digest-nudge.ts`; now it's one place. Documented the invariant in `.agents/playbook.md` along with the data-plane invariant about `MemberContext` fields surfacing to both the rules engine and `formatMemberContextForPrompt`.
