TMP: publisher-owned TMPX macro mapping and split providerŌåÆrouter / routerŌåÆpublisher response schemas.

Provider-to-router responses now carry `tmpx_values: [string]` ŌĆö an ordered list of opaque URL-safe TMPX chunks. Publisher-local destination names (ad-server macros, GAM key-values, VAST URL macros, DOOH play-log fields) MUST NOT travel between provider and router. Router-to-publisher responses reshape `tmpx_providers[provider_id]` to `{ values: [string] }`, preserving the emitting provider's chunk order. Position within `values[]` is the chunk's identity; the publisher's deployment configuration (`tmpx_macro_mapping`, captured in the new `publisher-tmpx-config.json`) resolves each `(provider_id, index)` to the local destination on that surface. Providers no longer declare `tmpx_macros` on their registration entry ŌĆö naming is a publisher-side deployment concern.

**Breaking change to an experimental surface** (sanctioned by `x-status: experimental`). Restores the direction described in #2203 by removing publisher-local names from the untrusted-provider boundary. Also closes the cross-provider name-hijack surface tracked as #5945 by construction ŌĆö the router never accepts destination names from providers.

Schemas: `trusted-match/identity-match-response.json` is now the routerŌåÆpublisher shape only (`tmpx_providers[provider_id].values`). New `trusted-match/provider-identity-match-response.json` captures the providerŌåÆrouter shape (`tmpx_values`). New `trusted-match/publisher-tmpx-config.json` captures the publisher-owned mapping shape. `trusted-match/provider-registration.json` drops the provider-declared `tmpx_macros` list. Spec narrative: IdentityMatchResponse field table splits per hop, a new `┬¦Publisher TMPX macro mapping` section walks the configuration shape and adapter obligations, and the router-architecture and inventory-specific-behavior sections are rewritten to consume ordered values rather than provider-emitted names.

**Migration.** Providers that emitted `tmpx_macros[{name,value}]` on their identity-match response emit `tmpx_values[value]` in the same order. Providers that registered a `tmpx_macros` list drop it from `provider-registration.json`. Routers that produced `tmpx_providers[provider_id].macros[{name,value}]` produce `tmpx_providers[provider_id].values[value]` in the same order. Publishers configure `tmpx_macro_mapping` in their deployment configuration to resolve each `(provider_id, index)` to the local destination. The legacy singular `tmpx` field remains supported through 3.x (removed in 4.0). The prior experimental shape from #5729 shipped only in 3.1.1ŌĆō3.1.4, days before this change; adopters (if any) migrate by walking `values[]` and looking up the destination in their publisher configuration instead of reading the wire-side `name`.
