# AgenticAdvertising.Org Membership Agreement

This Membership Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of the Effective Date (defined below) by and between the AgenticAdvertising.Org, a nonprofit corporation organized under the laws of the State of Delaware (the "Foundation"), and the undersigned entity ("Member"). The Foundation and Member are referred to herein individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

## 1. Purpose

The Foundation has been organized to promote the common business interests of participants in the agentic advertising industry by developing, maintaining, and advancing open technical standards that enable interoperability of artificial intelligence-driven advertising technologies, such as the Advertising Context Protocol (AdCP, an open technical standard that unifies advertising workflows across platforms); fostering collaboration among agentic advertising industry stakeholders; and supporting education and advocacy for the agentic advertising industry. In addition to the Foundation's Bylaws, Certificate of Incorporation, and applicable corporate policies adopted by the Board (collectively, the "**Policies**"), this Agreement governs Member's participation in the Foundation, including rights, obligations, and use of intellectual property.

## 2. Membership

**2.1 Eligibility.** Consistent with the Foundation's Bylaws, Certificate of Incorporation, and the Policies, membership is open to individuals or legal entities that: a) meet the qualifications and requirements specified for the applicable membership class under the Bylaws and any policies adopted by the Board; and b) support the Purposes and Mission of the Corporation as set forth in the Bylaws. All applications for membership are subject to Board review and approval.

**2.2 Admission.** Membership commences upon (i) execution of this Agreement by the Member and the Foundation, and (ii) receipt of applicable dues.

**2.3 Rights and Privileges.** Member shall have the rights stipulated in the Foundation's Bylaws, the Certificate of Incorporation, and the Policies. Member shall have the privileges provided for pursuant to the Policies adopted by the Board from time to time.

**2.4 Bylaws and Policies; Definitions.** The Member acknowledges receipt of the Foundation's Certificate of Incorporation, Bylaws, and Policies, and agrees to abide by the Bylaws and all duly adopted Policies, as amended from time to time by the Foundation's Board of Directors in accordance with law. Terms used in this Agreement have the same meaning as in the Foundation's Bylaws. In the event of any conflict between this Agreement and the Bylaws, the Bylaws shall govern.

**2.5 Designated Representative.** As stipulated in the Foundation's Bylaws, the individual who registered for membership at AgenticAdvertising.Org and agreed to these terms (or a subsequent individual appointed by Member by notice to and approval by AgenticAdvertising.Org) is the Member's Designated Representative for purpose of receiving notice under this Agreement and to exercise the Member's right to vote with the Foundation. If Member fails to designate a Designated Representative, notice may be sent to the Member at its business address of record.

## 3. Membership Dues

**3.1 Dues Schedule.** Member shall pay annual membership dues, assessments, and fees as determined by the Board.

**3.2 Payment Terms.** Dues are invoiced annually, payable within thirty (30) days of invoice date. Dues are non-refundable.

**3.3 Delinquency.** A Member more than sixty (60) days in arrears may have rights suspended until payment is made or its membership terminated.

## 4. Intellectual Property and Confidentiality

**4.1 IPR Policy.** Member hereby agrees to the Foundation's Intellectual Property Rights Policy (as updated from time to time), which is hereby incorporated by reference (the "IPR Policy"). Capitalized terms used but not defined in this Section 4 will have the meanings ascribed to them in the IPR Policy.

**4.2 Confidentiality.** "**Confidential Information**" means any non-public information made available in connection with Member's participation in the Foundation, which a reasonable person would understand to be confidential, including patent disclosures and Draft Specifications, unreleased Final Specifications and other unreleased Collective Works. Confidential Information does not include information that is publicly known through no fault of Member, lawfully received from a third party without restriction or independently developed. Member will use Confidential Information only to participate in Foundation activities, not disclose Confidential Information to any third party (except to its representatives who need to know it for those purposes and are bound by similar confidentiality obligations) and protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses for its own similar information (and in any event reasonable care). Member may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law, but will give prompt notice to the Foundation and limit disclosure to what is legally required.

## 5. Use of Names and Trademarks

**5.1 Foundation Marks.** The Foundation owns all rights in the "AgenticAdvertising.Org" "AdCP" and "Ad Context Protocol" names, logos, certification marks, and related brand identifiers (the "Foundation Marks").

**5.2 Member Use.** Subject to compliance with the IPR Policy, Member may use the Foundation Marks solely to identify itself as a member of the Foundation or as otherwise contemplated by the IPR Policy, including that any use of the "AdCP-Compliant" or similar designations must be in compliance with the IPR Policy.

## 6. Governance & Participation

**6.1 Working Groups.** Members may participate in Working Groups and other convenings pursuant to the Foundation's Bylaws and the Policies. Member shall have one vote in matters submitted to a membership vote as provided in the Bylaws, Certificate, or by law.

**6.2 Board of Directors.** Governance of the Foundation shall be vested in a Board of Directors elected in accordance with the Foundation's Bylaws.

**6.3 Policies.** Member agrees to comply with the Foundation's Bylaws, the Policies, and antitrust compliance guidelines as may be adopted from time to time.

## 7. Term and Termination

**7.1 Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for one (1) year, automatically renewing for successive one-year terms unless either Party provides thirty (30) days' prior written notice of non-renewal.

**7.2 Suspension and Termination by the Foundation.** The Foundation may suspend or terminate this Agreement upon written notice if Member (i) fails to pay dues within sixty (60) days of invoice, (ii) violates the IPR Policy or this Agreement, (iii) misuses Foundation Marks, (iv) engages in conduct materially detrimental to the Foundation; (v) or as otherwise determined in the Board's discretion in compliance with the Foundation's Bylaws and the Policies.

**7.3 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Member shall immediately cease use of the Marks. Member's accrued obligations prior to termination, including unpaid membership dues, assessments, and IP licensing commitments under the IPR Policy, shall survive. After termination, the Member shall have no further rights or privileges with the Foundation.

## 8. Confidentiality

Confidential information disclosed in the course of participation in Foundation activities shall be governed by the confidentiality provisions of the IPR Policy.

## 9. Compliance with Laws

Member shall comply with all applicable laws, including antitrust and competition laws, and agrees not to use Foundation participation as a forum for discussing competitively sensitive information.

## 10. Limitation of Liability

ALL CONTRIBUTIONS, SPECIFICATIONS, AND OTHER MATERIALS ARE PROVIDED "AS IS." NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS AGREEMENT, WHETHER BASED IN CONTRACT, TORT, OR OTHERWISE, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

## 11. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed under the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflicts of law principles. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in Delaware.

## 12. Miscellaneous

**12.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the Foundation's Bylaws and the Policies, and the IPR Policy, constitute the entire agreement between the Parties regarding Member's membership in the Foundation.

**12.2 Amendments.** Amendments require written approval by both Parties, except that the Foundation may adopt or amend policies applicable to all Members generally.

**12.3 Assignment.** Member may not assign this Agreement without prior written consent of the Foundation.

**12.4 Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts and delivered electronically, each of which shall be deemed an original.

**12.5 Authority.** Member represents and warrants that it is authorized to enter into this Agreement. The undersigned person represents and warrants that such person is authorized to sign this Agreement on behalf of the Member.

## 13. Effective Date

The Effective Date of this Agreement shall be the date of last signature below.
