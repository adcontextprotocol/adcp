Alex runs media operations at a mid-size agency. Her team manages campaigns across twelve publishers — CTV, display, audio, retail media, social. Each platform has its own API, its own dashboard, its own way of handling creatives, targeting, and reporting. Adding a thirteenth publisher means building a thirteenth integration from scratch.

Her team is good. But scaling to twenty platforms without doubling headcount isn't an execution problem — it's a protocol problem. Every platform speaks a different language.

## One protocol, every platform

<img src="/images/walkthrough/adcp-02-one-protocol.png" alt="A hexagonal protocol hub at center with clean teal radial lines connecting to six platform icons — a TV, a phone, a laptop, a billboard, a shopping cart, and a podcast microphone — each connection is identical and standardized" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '12px', marginBottom: '1rem' }} />

AdCP (Ad Context Protocol) replaces platform-specific integrations with a single open standard. Instead of learning each platform's API, agents use the same tasks everywhere:

| What you need to do | AdCP task | Works across |
|---|---|---|
| Find inventory | `get_products` | Every publisher and SSP |
| Buy media | `create_media_buy` | Every ad server and platform |
| Build creatives | `build_creative` | Every creative tool |
| Activate audiences | `activate_signal` | Every data provider |
| Check compliance | `check_governance` | Every governance vendor |

One integration. Every platform. The same JSON schema whether you're buying CTV inventory or activating retail media audiences.

## Specialized agents collaborate

<img src="/images/walkthrough/adcp-03-agents.png" alt="Five specialized robots collaborate around a shared workspace — a discovery robot with a magnifying glass, a creative robot with a paintbrush, a governance robot with a shield, a signals robot with an antenna, and an orchestrator robot coordinating them all from the center" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '12px', marginBottom: '1rem' }} />

AdCP doesn't assume a single AI handles everything. Specialized agents handle what they're best at:

- **Media buying agents** discover inventory and execute campaigns
- **Creative agents** generate and adapt ads across formats
- **Signals agents** find and activate audiences
- **Governance agents** enforce brand safety and compliance
- **Orchestrators** coordinate the workflow and make sure humans approve what matters

These agents communicate over two transport protocols: **MCP** (for AI assistants calling tools) and **A2A** (for agent-to-agent collaboration). Same tasks, same schemas, different transport.

## Brief to live ads

<img src="/images/walkthrough/adcp-04-workflow.png" alt="A horizontal flow showing a person writing a brief at a desk on the left, then agents in the middle producing different ad formats — video, display, audio — and on the right, finished ads running on screens across a city skyline" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '12px', marginBottom: '1rem' }} />

Here's what Alex's team does now:

1. **Write a brief**: "Find premium video inventory on sports publishers for Q2 with a $50K budget"
2. **Agents discover options**: `get_products` goes to every connected seller simultaneously
3. **Compare proposals**: Products come back in a standard format — pricing, forecasts, targeting — all comparable
4. **Agents build creatives**: `build_creative` adapts assets to each seller's formats
5. **Approve and launch**: `create_media_buy` executes across platforms in one call
6. **Monitor delivery**: `get_media_buy_delivery` aggregates performance from every seller into one view

Each step uses a standard AdCP task with a JSON Schema-defined request and response. No platform-specific code. No manual translation between systems.

## Governance watches everything

<img src="/images/walkthrough/adcp-05-governance.png" alt="A guardian robot at a checkpoint inspects campaign blueprints — approving compliant ones with green stamps and routing a flagged one along a glowing path to a human reviewer sitting nearby" style={{ width: '100%', borderRadius: '12px', marginBottom: '1rem' }} />

When AI agents spend money autonomously, trust requires structure. AdCP's governance layer provides it:

- **Before a campaign launches**: `check_governance` validates budget limits, brand safety, and regulatory compliance
- **If something exceeds authority**: The governance agent escalates to a human — your team approves, not the AI
- **While campaigns run**: Governance agents monitor delivery against approved parameters
- **After delivery**: `get_plan_audit_logs` provides a complete decision trail — who proposed what, who approved it, what actually ran

Governance isn't a gate that slows things down. It's the safety net that lets you give agents more autonomy over time.

## How it works

AdCP defines **tasks** — discrete advertising operations like `get_products`, `create_media_buy`, or `activate_signal`. Each task has a JSON Schema-defined request and response. Agents call tasks through two transport protocols:

- **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** — Direct integration with AI assistants. An MCP server exposes AdCP tasks as tools that any MCP-compatible client can call.
- **A2A (Agent-to-Agent Protocol)** — Multi-agent workflows where specialized agents collaborate on complex campaigns.

The same task definitions work over both transports. Build once, serve both.

## Protocol domains

AdCP 3.0 covers eight domains:

| Domain | What it does | Key tasks | Access |
|--------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| **Accounts** | Commercial identity and billing | `sync_accounts`, `list_accounts` | MCP / A2A |
| **Media Buy** | Inventory discovery and campaign execution | `get_products`, `create_media_buy` | MCP / A2A |
| **Creative** | Creative generation, catalogs, and AI provenance | `build_creative`, `sync_creatives`, `sync_catalogs` | MCP / A2A |
| **Signals** | Audience and targeting data | `get_signals`, `activate_signal` | MCP / A2A |
| **Governance** | Brand suitability, compliance, and provenance verification | `create_property_list`, `calibrate_content` | MCP / A2A |
| **Sponsored Intelligence** | Conversational brand experiences | `si_initiate_session`, `si_send_message` | MCP / A2A |
| **Brand Protocol** | Brand identity, rights licensing, and creative approval | `get_brand_identity`, `acquire_rights` | HTTP / MCP / A2A |
| **Registry** | Entity resolution and agent discovery | `/api/brands/resolve`, `/api/registry/agents` | REST API |

These domains work together. A typical workflow: resolve a brand via **Brand Protocol**, establish billing via **Accounts**, discover inventory via **Media Buy**, find audiences via **Signals**, check brand suitability via **Governance**, build ads via **Creative**, and execute across platforms.

## For platform providers

AI is buying ads. Make sure it can buy yours.

If you operate a DSP, SSP, publisher, data platform, creative platform, governance service, or any ad tech solution, AdCP lets AI agents discover and transact with your platform. To get started:

1. **Implement an AdCP agent** — Expose your platform's capabilities as AdCP tasks over MCP or A2A. Start with `get_adcp_capabilities`.
2. **Publish your adagents.json** — Declare your properties and authorized agents so buyers can discover you.
3. **Test your implementation** — Validate with [Addie](https://adcontextprotocol.org/chat), the [client SDKs](/docs/building/schemas-and-sdks), or the [CLI](/docs/quickstart).

Implement the protocol domains relevant to your business:
- **Publishers and SSPs**: [Media Buy](/docs/media-buy) and [adagents.json](/docs/governance/property)
- **Data providers**: [Signals](/docs/signals/overview) and [data provider guide](/docs/signals/data-providers)
- **Creative platforms**: [Creative](/docs/creative)
- **Governance vendors**: [Governance protocol](/docs/governance/overview)
- **Brands**: [Brand Protocol](/docs/brand-protocol) and [brand.json](/docs/brand-protocol/brand-json)

## For advertisers and agencies

Run campaigns across more platforms without scaling your team.

AdCP-enabled agents work across all your media partners through a single interface — the same tasks buy CTV inventory, activate audience data, and manage creatives regardless of which platform you're working with.

1. **Check platform support** — See which of your media partners support AdCP, or browse the [registry](/docs/registry).
2. **Try it with Addie** — [Ask Addie](https://adcontextprotocol.org/chat) to walk you through the protocol — no code required.
3. **Connect with your team** — Share the [quickstart guide](/docs/quickstart) with your technical team to integrate client libraries.
